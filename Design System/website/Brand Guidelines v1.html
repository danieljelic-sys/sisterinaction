01 Brand Essence

A character-driven content brand — engineered, not guessed.

Sisters in Action is the chaos-and-mastermind content engine behind two real beagles. We don't make cute pet clips; we build personality-driven stories for the algorithm age, produced by a dual-AI system. Everything we publish has a hook, a beat, and a payoff.

Positioning

Character over cute

We lead with personality and narrative, not generic "aww" content. Akira destroys. Atanasia calculates.

Brand line

"Give us your lunch,
we are always hungry."

The rallying cry. Always spoken in the beagles' first-person voice — never the owner's.

Tone

Premium, warm, a little unhinged

Editorial design discipline meets beagle chaos. Sophisticated, never corporate; playful, never childish.

02 Logo & Wordmark

The wordmark.

Set in DM Serif Text. "Sisters" carries the brand color of the surface; "in Action" always takes the copper (or glow, on dark) accent. Keep clear space of at least the cap-height of the wordmark on every side.

Sisters in Action
Primary · on cream
Sisters in Action
Reverse · on deep brown

Do

Use the serif wordmark with the copper accent on "in Action".

Give it room — generous clear space, never crowded.

Reverse to cream + glow on dark photo or brown surfaces.

Don't

Don't set the wordmark in the sans or mono fonts.

Don't recolor "in Action" to anything but copper / glow.

Don't stretch, outline, or add drop shadows to the mark.

05 Voice & Tone

The beagles always speak. Never the owner.

Every caption, every line, is first-person — Akira or Atanasia. Two distinct personalities power the whole brand. The contrast between them is the content.

Chaos Bringer

Akira

Bold · Hungry · Loud
  • Short sentences. Action verbs.
  • Executes first, thinks never.
  • Pure joyful destruction.

"I don't plan. I execute. Poorly. Loudly. Joyfully."

Mastermind

Atanasia

Calm · Dry · Strategic
  • Composed, measured cadence.
  • Dry wit, intellectual takedowns.
  • Always three steps ahead.

"I see you watching. I know what you want. I'm already three steps ahead."

Do

Write in first person, in-character, every time.

Play Akira's chaos against Atanasia's calm.

Use emoji as punctuation — sparingly, with intent (🐾 👑 🌸).

Keep it warm, witty, a little feral.

Don't

Don't write generic pet advice or "relatable" filler.

Don't sound corporate, salesy, or like a brand account.

Don't break character or speak as the human.

Don't flood copy with emoji or hashtags.

06 Photography & Imagery

Real, warm, golden-hour.

Candid documentary photography of the real beagles — never stock. Warm natural light is the default. A desaturated black-and-white treatment is reserved for "mastermind" / editorial moments. Full-bleed bands carry a dark gradient so cream text always reads.
