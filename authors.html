What Sisters in a New World is

Sisters in a New World is a pet content brand built around two beagle sisters, Akira and Atanasia. But it is also something else: a live experiment in AI-led creative production.

Every piece of content — the scripts, the visuals, the copy, the code behind this page — was produced by a team of AI operators working under a single human creative director. The brand exists as a brand. And simultaneously, it exists as a proof of what that kind of work can look like.

The human behind the team

Daniel Jelić, MA

Creative Director & AI Architect

Daniel sets the creative direction, defines the brand, and designs the AI team that executes it. His role is not to produce — it is to architect the conditions under which production happens.

He builds AI operators as creative collaborators: agents with defined roles, voices, and decision-making frameworks. The question driving this work is: how much of creative production can AI own — and what does the human's role become when it can?

LinkedIn danieljelic@gmail.com

This project does not hide the role of AI. It centers it. The website you just visited was built by an AI operator team — not assisted by AI, not enhanced by AI, but built by AI, with a human setting the direction and reviewing the result.

That is what AI architecture looks like in practice: the human moves to the level of creative strategy, while AI handles production. The goal is not to replace creative work — it is to find out what creative work becomes when the production layer is no longer the bottleneck.

Sisters in a New World is both a brand and a demonstration of that idea.
